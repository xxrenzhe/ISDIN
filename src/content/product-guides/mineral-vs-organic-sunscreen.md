Sunscreen filters absorb ultraviolet energy and convert it into other forms of energy; mineral filters also scatter a small portion. The familiar claim that mineral sunscreen only reflects while organic sunscreen only absorbs is too simplistic.

## What mineral means in the United States

Mineral formulas use zinc oxide, titanium dioxide or both as active ingredients. Zinc oxide can provide broad UVA and UVB coverage. Titanium dioxide is effective across UVB and part of UVA, so formulas must be evaluated as a tested whole.

Mineral products can be comfortable for some sensitive users and begin protecting when an even film is formed. Their common drawbacks are visible cast, dryness and a heavier texture. Tints can reduce cast, though one tint will not match every skin tone.

## What organic filters offer

Organic filters include avobenzone, octocrylene, octisalate, homosalate and others depending on the market. Combining filters allows formulators to create broad-spectrum protection and can enable lighter, clearer finishes.

Some people experience eye sting or sensitivity with a particular filter or vehicle. That does not make the entire category unsuitable. Fragrance, alcohol, preservatives and the rest of the formula also affect tolerance.

## Choose the tested product, not a filter tribe

Prioritize:

1. Broad-spectrum SPF 30 or higher.
2. Water resistance when swimming or sweating.
3. A texture and tint that support sufficient application.
4. Compatibility with the rest of the routine.
5. A label appropriate to your market.

Do not assume a long active-ingredient list is stronger, or that a high zinc percentage predicts a better UVA result without the product's finished-formula testing.

## Use-case comparison

| Need               | Useful starting point                                      | Watch for               |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------- |
| No visible cast    | Clear organic or well-matched tinted formula               | Eye sting, pilling      |
| Very reactive skin | Fragrance-free mineral formula                             | Dryness, tint range     |
| Outdoor sport      | Broad-spectrum, water-resistant formula in either category | Reapplication and sweat |
| Makeup wear        | Thin fluid or primer-like texture                          | Applying too little     |

The better category is the one that meets protection needs and stays usable on your skin.

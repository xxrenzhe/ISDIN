Sunlight reaches skin as a range of wavelengths. For an everyday sunscreen decision, the useful distinction is simple: ultraviolet B is strongly associated with sunburn, while ultraviolet A penetrates more deeply and contributes to premature visible aging. Both can contribute to skin cancer risk.

## SPF answers only part of the question

Sun protection factor, or SPF, is primarily a measure of protection against UVB under controlled test conditions. A higher number can indicate more UVB protection when the product is applied at the tested amount, but SPF does not tell you how comfortable the product will feel, how evenly you will apply it or whether you will reapply it.

In the United States, look for the words **broad spectrum**. That label indicates that the formula has passed a test for UVA protection in addition to its stated UVB protection. It is more useful than trying to infer coverage from one familiar filter name.

## Why UVA changes the daily-use conversation

UVA is present throughout daylight hours and can pass through window glass more readily than UVB. Exposure depends on season, latitude, cloud cover, altitude, time outdoors and the glass itself. That does not mean every indoor moment carries the same risk. It means a daily routine should reflect actual exposure rather than treating sunscreen as a beach-only product.

For someone managing uneven tone or visible photoaging, consistent broad-spectrum protection also helps prevent ongoing exposure from competing with the rest of the routine.

## The label is not the whole routine

A sunscreen can only perform as labeled when enough is applied and the film stays reasonably intact. Use these checks:

- Choose broad-spectrum SPF 30 or higher for routine exposed-skin use.
- Apply before sustained exposure so you are not rushing outdoors with missed areas.
- Reapply after swimming, heavy sweating, towel drying and at the interval on the label.
- Add shade, clothing, a hat and sunglasses when exposure is prolonged.

No sunscreen blocks every ultraviolet ray. The most reliable plan combines a product you can apply generously with sensible exposure reduction.

## A clearer purchase test

Before comparing antioxidants, elegant textures or marketing terms, verify four basics: broad-spectrum labeling, suitable SPF, water-resistance when the situation requires it and a texture you will use in the needed amount. Those criteria make the product more likely to work in real life.

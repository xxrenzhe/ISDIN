Uneven tone can have many causes, including sun exposure, marks left after inflammation and hormonal patterns such as melasma. A cosmetic routine cannot determine the cause of every patch. Seek professional assessment for a new, changing, irregular or symptomatic spot.

## Control the driver first

Dark marks can deepen with ultraviolet exposure, and visible light can matter for some pigmentation patterns. Broad-spectrum sunscreen is therefore part of the treatment logic, not a decorative final step. A tinted sunscreen containing iron oxides may be worth discussing for visible-light-sensitive pigmentation, especially melasma.

Also reduce avoidable inflammation. Picking blemishes, scrubbing, over-exfoliating and pushing through persistent irritation can create more unevenness than an active product resolves.

## Choose one targeted active

Several cosmetic ingredients may support a tone-focused routine:

- **Niacinamide** can support barrier function and may help the appearance of uneven tone.
- **Tranexamic acid** is used in topical formulas aimed at discoloration; evidence is promising but formulations and study designs vary.
- **Retinoids** can improve cell turnover and support broader photoaging goals, with irritation as the main practical limit.
- **Vitamin C** can provide antioxidant support, but stability and packaging deserve attention.

Introduce one option at a time. That makes it easier to understand both benefit and irritation.

## Use a realistic timeline

Pigment does not move at the speed of a marketing animation. Consistency over eight to twelve weeks is a more useful assessment window for many cosmetic products, and some marks take longer. Photograph progress only under comparable lighting.

A stable routine of sunscreen, gentle care and one tolerable active usually gives a clearer answer than a rotating set of brightening products.

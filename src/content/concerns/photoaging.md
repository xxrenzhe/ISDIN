Photoaging is the visible effect of repeated ultraviolet exposure layered on top of normal chronological change. It may show up as uneven pigmentation, rougher texture, fine lines, reduced elasticity or persistent areas of redness. These signs are not a diagnosis, and sudden or changing spots should be assessed by a qualified clinician.

## Start by reducing new exposure

The foundation is not a complicated corrective serum. It is consistent broad-spectrum sun protection paired with shade and protective clothing when exposure is significant. This step matters because continued ultraviolet exposure can keep driving the same processes a treatment routine is trying to address.

For many people, a daily facial sunscreen needs to be cosmetically workable: it should spread evenly, sit comfortably around the eyes, cooperate with makeup when relevant and be easy to reapply. A technically impressive formula that stays unused is a poor fit.

## Add one treatment objective

Retinoids have some of the strongest evidence among topical cosmetic ingredients for improving visible fine lines and uneven texture over time. They can also irritate, especially when introduced too quickly. Begin with a low frequency, use a plain moisturizer and adjust according to tolerance.

Vitamin C products may provide antioxidant support and can help with the appearance of uneven tone, but formulation stability varies. Packaging, derivative, concentration and storage all affect whether a product remains practical.

Neither category replaces sunscreen, and neither promises to erase established change.

## Keep the barrier in the plan

Dryness and irritation make an otherwise sensible routine harder to sustain. A gentle cleanser, moisturizer and deliberate pacing can be more valuable than stacking several active products. When skin is persistently stinging, peeling or inflamed, pause the new treatment and simplify.

## A three-part framework

1. **Protect:** broad-spectrum sunscreen plus exposure-reducing habits.
2. **Treat:** one evidence-aligned active selected for the main concern.
3. **Support:** cleansing and moisturizing that preserve comfort.

Assess visible change over months, not days. Compare under similar lighting and avoid before-and-after conclusions based on a single flattering photograph.

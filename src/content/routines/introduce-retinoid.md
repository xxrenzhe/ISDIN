The most useful retinoid schedule is not the fastest one. It is the schedule your skin can sustain.

## Before the first night

Stabilize the basics: gentle cleanser, moisturizer and daily sunscreen. Pause leave-on exfoliating acids and scrubs for the first few weeks. Patch test the new product according to its directions.

## Weeks one and two

Use the retinoid on two non-consecutive nights. Cleanse, let skin dry and apply a pea-sized amount across the face. Avoid eyelid margins, corners of the nose and lips. Follow with moisturizer.

Very dry or reactive skin can use the moisturizer sandwich: moisturizer, retinoid, then another light moisturizer layer. This may reduce intensity without requiring a second treatment product.

## Weeks three and four

If there is no persistent burning, marked redness or cracking, consider every third night. Do not increase both amount and frequency at once. Mild short-lived dryness can happen, but ongoing inflammation means the schedule is too aggressive.

## After one month

Stay at the lowest frequency that feels comfortable. There is no requirement to reach nightly use. Visible changes in texture and fine lines typically require months of consistency.

## What not to stack at first

Keep strong exfoliating acids, benzoyl peroxide and other retinoids out of the same routine until tolerance is established and the product directions support the combination. Vitamin C can remain in the morning if comfortable.

Avoid cosmetic retinoids during pregnancy out of caution and ask a clinician about use during breastfeeding, eczema flares or alongside prescription treatments.

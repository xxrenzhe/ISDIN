Sensitive skin is a description, not one diagnosis. When skin is dry, tight or easily irritated, a short routine makes triggers easier to identify.

## Morning

1. **Rinse or cleanse gently.** Use lukewarm water and a fragrance-free cleanser only if needed.
2. **Moisturize.** Look for glycerin, ceramides and emollient lipids in a texture that relieves tightness.
3. **Protect.** Apply broad-spectrum SPF 30 or higher. Mineral filters are not automatically gentler, so judge the complete formula and eye-area comfort.

## Evening

Cleanse to remove sunscreen, then apply moisturizer while skin is slightly damp. A thin occlusive layer can help the driest areas, but heavy ointment is not comfortable or necessary for everyone.

## Remove common friction

Pause scrubs, cleansing brushes, fragranced oils and leave-on acids while the barrier is unsettled. Shorten hot showers and avoid repeated washing. Patch test new products on a small area for several days.

## Add treatment only after stability

Once skin has felt comfortable for at least two weeks, introduce one treatment at low frequency. A retinoid might begin once weekly; an exfoliant may not be needed at all. Keep the rest of the routine unchanged so any reaction has a clear likely cause.

Persistent itching, swelling, cracking, oozing or a rash deserves professional assessment. A minimal cosmetic routine can support comfort, but it cannot identify eczema, allergy or another skin condition.

Vitamin C and retinal do not inherently cancel each other out. The more useful question is whether two active products remain stable and comfortable in the same session.

## The simplest split

Use vitamin C in the morning under broad-spectrum sunscreen and retinal at night before moisturizer. This arrangement gives each product a clear role and reduces the chance of pilling or cumulative stinging.

Morning:

1. Gentle cleanse or rinse.
2. Vitamin C serum.
3. Moisturizer if needed.
4. Broad-spectrum sunscreen.

Evening:

1. Gentle cleanser.
2. Retinal on dry skin.
3. Moisturizer.

## When the same routine may work

An experienced user may tolerate both at night, especially if the vitamin C uses a gentle derivative. Apply according to product texture and directions. There is rarely a compelling reason to force this combination when separate use works.

L-ascorbic acid formulas often have a low pH and may sting. Retinal can also cause dryness. Using both at once may increase irritation even though the ingredients are chemically compatible in ordinary use.

## Troubleshooting

If skin burns or peels, pause retinal until comfortable, then restart at a lower frequency. If products pill, reduce the amount and separate them by time of day. Do not solve pilling by rubbing harder.

The pair supports a broader photoaging routine, but sunscreen remains the protective foundation and visible improvement still depends on months of consistent use.

Fragrance does not need a special display case to be treated well. It does benefit from being kept away from the parts of a home that change temperature and light most dramatically. The simplest useful approach is to store bottles upright in a cool, dry cabinet or drawer and keep the cap on when they are not in use.

## Choose consistency over display

Bathroom shelves and sunny windowsills can be convenient, but they also expose a bottle to changing humidity, heat and direct light. A dresser drawer or closed cabinet is usually easier on a fragrance and easier to keep consistent. Keep the original box when it is practical, especially for a bottle you use only occasionally.

Do not turn this into a rule against using what you own. A favorite bottle belongs where you will reach for it. The point is to avoid leaving it in the harshest conditions day after day.

## Keep the bottle simple

Close the cap, avoid repeatedly decanting a fragrance into decorative containers and keep bottles upright to reduce preventable mess. If the color, scent or texture changes markedly, pause before using it on sensitive skin. Product labels can provide useful handling information; they cannot make a bottle impervious to poor storage.

## Buy in a size you can enjoy

For a scent you are still learning, a smaller size can be more useful than a large bottle stored for years. Sampling, wearing it in different settings and choosing a size you can realistically finish makes fragrance feel less like an object to preserve and more like something to use.

Eryfotona Actinica and Eryfotona Ageless are fluid mineral sunscreens positioned around daily photoprotection. Their biggest practical difference is visible immediately: Actinica is untinted, while Ageless is tinted.

## BES3 verdict

Choose Actinica when you want an untinted mineral SPF 50+ and prefer a simpler appearance-focused brief. Choose Ageless when a tint and added cosmetic photoaging ingredients fit your routine. Neither tint nor a proprietary complex replaces correct sunscreen application.

The zinc percentages cannot be used to rank protection on their own. SPF and broad-spectrum results belong to the tested finished formula.

## Understanding the repair language

ISDIN says both formulas use plankton-derived DNA Repairsomes and makes help-repair claims around existing sun damage. Treat this as brand-specific supporting technology, not permission to describe a sunscreen as reversing all photodamage. Prevention of ongoing exposure remains the reliable role of daily SPF.

Ageless adds peptides and cosmetic claims around fine lines and wrinkles. Those ingredients may be useful supporting features, but the product's regulated sunscreen performance is the first reason to buy it.

## Texture and tint tradeoffs

Fluid mineral sunscreens can settle better than thick pastes, yet zinc oxide may still leave visible residue or emphasize dry patches. Actinica's untinted finish may be more visible on deeper skin tones. Ageless may reduce cast, but one tint cannot match every complexion.

Test a full application in daylight and allow the film to set. Check current water-resistance and reapplication directions on the label, especially for outdoor use.

Retinal Advanced is ISDIN's dual-phase night serum built around retinaldehyde. The package dispenses a retinal phase and a soothing phase to be mixed at application. ISDIN also lists melatonin and bakuchiol among the featured ingredients.

## BES3 verdict

This product is for a user intentionally adding a retinoid for visible fine lines, texture or uneven tone and willing to increase frequency gradually. It is not a low-attention moisturizer. Sensitive or retinoid-naive skin should treat the soothing phase as support, not a guarantee against irritation.

## Why retinal matters

Retinaldehyde converts to retinoic acid in one skin-conversion step, while retinol requires two. That chemistry makes retinal an interesting cosmetic retinoid, but concentration, stability and delivery still determine the final result.

ISDIN cites company studies in 34 participants for several visible outcomes. The official page identifies these as data on file. BES3 treats them as brand evidence rather than independent replication, and the small study size limits broad conclusions.

## A cautious introduction

Use at night on dry skin. Mix the two dispensed phases as directed and apply a small amount across the face, avoiding eyelid margins and irritated areas.

- Weeks 1-2: one or two non-consecutive nights.
- Weeks 3-4: every third night if comfortable.
- After week 4: hold or increase slowly; nightly use is not mandatory.

Follow with moisturizer. Pause exfoliating acids while learning tolerance. Persistent burning, swelling or cracking means stop and allow skin to recover.

## Who should take extra care

People using prescription retinoids, managing eczema or receiving procedures should ask a qualified clinician before adding another active. Cosmetic retinoids are generally avoided during pregnancy out of caution.

Use broad-spectrum sunscreen each morning. A retinoid routine aimed at photoaging makes little sense without dependable protection from ongoing exposure.
